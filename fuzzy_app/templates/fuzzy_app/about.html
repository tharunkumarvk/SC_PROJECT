About This System

Tropical Disease Prediction using Fuzzy Logic + Random Forest Consensus

This system predicts the most likely tropical disease from patient symptom inputs using a dual-model consensus approach for improved accuracy and reliability.

Scope

Covers 10 conditions — 8 major tropical diseases recognized by the World Health Organization (WHO) plus Common Cold and Influenza for realistic differential diagnosis — assessed via {{ symptom_count }} clinical symptoms. All symptom data is sourced from WHO fact sheets, CDC guidelines, Harrison's Principles of Internal Medicine (21st Ed), and Manson's Tropical Diseases (24th Ed).

How It Works
  1. Enter symptoms — rate your symptoms on the provided scales (temperature for fever, 0-10 severity for others).
  2. Fuzzy Logic Analysis — symptoms are evaluated against clinically-derived fuzzy rules. Each disease has an expected symptom pattern with triangular/trapezoidal membership functions. A weighted pattern-matching score is computed.
  3. Random Forest Classification — a trained Random Forest model (150 trees, max depth 12) independently classifies the symptoms based on prevalence-generated training data derived from WHO/CDC clinical profiles with 5-fold cross-validation.
  4. Consensus Mechanism — both predictions are combined with dynamic weighting based on data completeness. When both models agree, confidence is higher. Disagreement triggers warnings.
